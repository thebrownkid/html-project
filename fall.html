Fall's Gentle Requiem

In the embrace of autumn, the world shifts,
A mosaic of crimson, gold, and deep auburn drifts,
Canopies once lush, now surrender with grace,
As leaves cascade, each finding its resting place.

Whispers of change rustle through the trees,
Tales of cycles, of life's great tapestries,
Every leaf a story, every hue a memory,
Marking moments of joy, hope, and reverie.

Rivers reflect the palette of the shifting sky,
Mirroring the world as it prepares its goodbye,
To the warmth, to the sun, to days long and clear,
Embracing the twilight of the year's final frontier.

Beneath the canopy, the earth wears a quilt,
Blanketed by leaves, in the glow of the sunset's gilt,
The winds carry secrets, ancient and profound,
Urging the heart to listen, to the world's changing sound.

In the heart of fall, there's a lesson to glean,
About embracing transitions, and the beauty unseen,
For in letting go, in the dance of the falling leaves,
Lies the promise of renewal, and all that life achieves.

So as autumn unfurls, with its tapestry so grand,
May we too find the strength to understand,
That change is but a passage, a doorway to the new,
And in letting go, we truly find our truest hue.
