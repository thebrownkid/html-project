Summer’s Lush Ballet

In the heart of summer, the world basks,
Under a dome of sapphire and shimmering rays,
Golden light paints every corner, every cranny,
As earth rejoices in a season of warmth and play.

Orchards laden with fruits of amber and ruby,
Nectarines blush, kissed by the sun’s tender glow,
Berries glisten, a mosaic of jeweled delight,
Promising sweet bursts with every bite they bestow.

Fields of corn stand tall, whispering tales of abundance,
Their tassels swaying like dancers in a midsummer dream,
Vines spiral and twirl, heavy with grapes so lush,
An ode to nature’s generosity, in every sunlit gleam.

Children’s laughter echoes, free and unbridled,
As they chase after dragonflies and taste nature’s treat,
Juice-streaked smiles, evidence of the season’s bounty,
Each bite a memory, every flavor so complete.

Evenings unveil a different shade of warmth,
Cicadas serenade the setting sun’s embrace,
Fireflies waltz, lanterns in the dimming light,
As stars begin their watch, adorning the night’s lace.

In the embrace of summer, the heart feels full,
With the richness of moments, both fleeting and grand,
For in this season of endless sun and ripe abundance,
Life flourishes, nurtured by nature’s generous hand.
