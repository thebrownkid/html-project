Winter’s Hushed Lullaby

In the arms of winter, the world grows still,
A canvas of white, shaped by nature's will,
Snowflakes descend, each a silent serenade,
Crafting landscapes in shimmering brocade.

Forests stand solemn, in crystalline attire,
Boughs bowing gently, as if to the frost’s sire,
Rivers pause, lulled into icy dreams,
Mirroring the heavens in their frozen beams.

Homes aglow with amber, a beacon in the night,
Smoke rising in spirals, painting tales of respite,
Families gather, sharing stories old and new,
As flames dance, casting shadows of golden hue.

In the quiet of winter, the earth takes a breath,
Embracing the stillness, the season of rest,
Where every snowflake, in its delicate descent,
Whispers of patience, and moments well-spent.

Footprints mark journeys across the pristine plane,
Yet, in time, the snow claims its realm once again,
In this world of silence, dreams take flight,
Guided by the stars, and the moon's gentle light.

As winter envelops, in its embrace so profound,
May our souls find peace, in the quiet all around,
For in this season of snowflakes and restful respite,
Lies the magic of reflection, and the promise of starlit nights.
